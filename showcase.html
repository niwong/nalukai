Teaching Kids Coding - Ally Nisenoff

After being so lucky in the opportunities I have had, I knew there had to be some way for me to give back. I noticed that there aren’t many opportunities for students to be exposed to coding anymore. I really wanted other kids to have the same opportunity with coding that I did. I took on creating an after school programming class for interested students. I am also collaborating with a teacher at Kealakehe Intermediate School to incorporate more computer programming into his classes. Before I attended Nalukai those tasks seemed impossible, but with all of the experiences I’ve gained (not to mention the connections) it all seems much more manageable.


Humans of Nalukai - Keona Conroy-Humphrey

While at Nalukai I was tasked with working on a media profile called Humans of Nalukai. This is based directly off of the project Humans of New York. Basically my job, along with my team, was to interview every student asking about a moment they truly cherished whether it has been at Nalukai or previously. Doing this project has opened my eyes to every single student experiences that they’ve had, which has helped me better connect with them.


Learning How to Code - Leilani Maui

When I started learning to code, I started with an online course called “Udemy”. Udemy is a website course that teaches you in step-by-step format. Udemy guides you and teaches you how to make games, starting with simple games, but progressively getting harder and more complicated.
The first course I completed is called “Number Wizard.” In Number Wizard, the computer is programmed to try and guess your number in a certain amount of attempts. If it guesses your number correctly within the maximum amount of attempts, the computer wins. If it doesn’t guess your number, you win!
Soon after I started, my Kumu found out about Nalukai Academy, and told me that I should apply, and so I did. I was so nervous but when I got to camp, my feelings about everything changed. I saw how much potential our generation has. I realized how much all of our skills go hand-in-hand in business and life. It opened my mind to trying new things. Now I’m expanding my knowledge much more and gaining new skills that are critical in life because of Nalukai. I’m even making new friends that share the same types of passions as I have.


3D Modeling on SketchUp - Makai Mossman

I always sketch out my newest designs in my sketchbooks. However, at Nalukai I was introduced to a 3D modeling program called “SketchUp” and I am absolutely in love with it! I can finally show the different dimensions of my designs! Whenever I can, I work on my latest 3D model. As of late, I’ve been obsessed with tanks so I’ve dedicated as much time as i can spare to modeling my own tank designs. SketchUp is pretty minor learning curve so it’s easy to figure out how to make awesome 3D models!


Nalukai Digital Media Classes - Mason Puett

Over the ten action-packed days, the classes at Nalukai stressed the importance of user empathy, expressing how the user’s needs altered ideation, prototyping, and beta-testing stages. This same principle translated to our applications of digital media, where we learned to leverage digital storytelling to engage our users. During the final website project, I took on the swing position, helping anyone who needed an extra hand. In this role, I took on a number of different tasks while we created our website, like putting together a video montage and organizing project management timelines.


Nalukai Project Management

At Nalukai you’re instructed on the basics of project management. Handling deadlines and interconnected teams is a difficult endeavor, but this lesson proved to be the most immediately useful as we worked towards completing the final website project.
